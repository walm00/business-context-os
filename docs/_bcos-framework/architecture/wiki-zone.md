# Wiki Zone Architecture

How `docs/_wiki/` works in CLEAR Context OS — what it holds, how content gets in, how it stays healthy, how its vocabularies grow as the business grows, and how it stays distinct from the other zones (data points, collections, inbox, planned, archive). Designed to be AI-friendly today and graph/RAG-ready tomorrow.

For the sibling zone mechanics see [`collections-zone.md`](./collections-zone.md) and [`content-routing.md`](./content-routing.md). For the philosophical placement of zones see [`system-design.md`](./system-design.md). For the canonical zone registry that names `wiki` alongside its eleven siblings, see [`context-zones.md`](./context-zones.md) — wiki is one zone among many; the registry is what every cross-zone consumer reads.

> **Net summary in one line:** keep pin-llm-wiki's clever mechanics (HTML-comment DSL, banner citations, three structural shapes, fetch protocols, soft-delete cascade), and replace its loose semantics (`tags`, `related`, `product`, version-less frontmatter, advisory-only lint) with BCOS's CLEAR ownership, frontmatter hook, propagation-based staleness, scheduled maintenance jobs, and a **schema-driven governance layer** so vocabularies grow with the business instead of being chiseled in stone.

---

## Why a Wiki Zone Exists

BCOS is built around **canonical, exclusively-owned data points**: each fact has exactly one home, and we link instead of duplicating. That model is excellent for *what is true* and terrible for *how to do things*.

Operational and explanatory knowledge has no home in the data-point model:

- A runbook for rebuilding `document-index.md` when frontmatter is corrupt
- A how-to for onboarding a new client to BCOS
- A glossary that defines "cluster," "data point," and "document" for newcomers
- A post-mortem on the March pricing migration incident
- A decision log explaining why we picked Postgres over Mongo in 2025
- A reformatted FAQ derived from the brand-voice data point
- An external source summary captured from a website, GitHub repo, or YouTube talk

These pages **explain**, **operate on**, or **summarize for skimming** the canonical truths in `docs/*.md`. They are derivative by design — they cite upstream data points, never replace them. That's what `docs/_wiki/` is for.

## Where Wiki Sits Among the Zones

Each underscore-prefixed zone in BCOS carries a different **truth-mode**. The wiki zone slots in alongside the others without overlap.

| Zone | Truth-mode | Form | Authoring | Mutability | "If I edit this, am I making it…" |
|------|-----------|------|-----------|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| `docs/*.md` | **Canonical** — source of truth | Data point with ownership spec | We write + maintain | Drifts; CLEAR-audited | …more *correct* (refining canonical truth) |
| `docs/_wiki/` | **Derivative** — explains canonical | Page with `builds-on:` upstream links | We write | Stale-prone; review-cycled | …more *clear* (refining explanation) |
| `docs/_collections/` | **Evidence** — verbatim artifact | File as-received (PDF, .docx, transcript) | Uploaded, not edited | Append-only | …more *wrong* (corrupting evidence) |
| `docs/_planned/` | **Hypothetical** — not real yet | Relaxed-schema doc | We write | Stable until promoted/dropped | …more *real* (premature; should stay tentative) |
| `docs/_archive/` | **Historical** — was real once | Frozen as-was | Don't edit | Immutable | …revisionist (corrupting history) |
| `docs/_inbox/` | **Unclassified** — pending triage | Raw text dump | Captured | Temporary | …a sign it should be triaged out |

**The four tightest distinctions:**

1. **Wiki vs. data point.** Data point: "Our target audience is small fintech teams in EU." Wiki: "How we run discovery calls to validate audience assumptions."
2. **Wiki vs. collection.** Collection: the signed Acme contract PDF. Wiki: a how-to titled "Reading Our Acme Contract."
3. **Wiki vs. `_planned/`.** `_planned/` = future business state. Wiki = current operational knowledge.
4. **Wiki vs. `_inbox/`.** `_inbox/` = temporary triage. Wiki = permanent with review cycle.

## Folder Layout

Pages flat under `_wiki/pages/`. Page-type is the **only** discriminator (frontmatter, not folder). External URL captures keep their own subdirectory because they have a distinct lifecycle (URL-fetched, structural shapes, refresh-must-rediscover applies).

```
docs/_wiki/
├── .config.yml                   # Wiki-zone runtime config (detail level, cadence, source types)
├── .schema.yml                   # Vocabulary registry (page-types, statuses, detail-levels — see "Schema and Governance")
├── README.md                     # Zone overview + decision tree (auto-maintained)
├── index.md                      # Page table — DERIVED ARTIFACT (regenerated by refresh_wiki_index.py; never hand-edited)
├── overview.md                   # Cross-page synthesis with [[wikilinks]]
├── log.md                        # Append-only ingest/refresh log
├── queue.md                      # Pending URLs (Path A) — renamed from pin-llm-wiki's inbox.md to disambiguate from docs/_inbox/
│
├── pages/                        # All internally-authored pages (flat; page-type discriminator in frontmatter)
│   ├── onboarding-flow.md        # page-type: how-to
│   ├── data-point-glossary.md    # page-type: glossary
│   ├── q1-pricing-incident.md    # page-type: post-mortem
│   └── …
│
├── source-summary/               # External URL captures (Path A only); has umbrella/sub/unified shape rules
│   ├── langchain-com.md
│   ├── langchain-com-langgraph.md
│   └── …
│
├── raw/
│   ├── web/<slug>.md             # Pin-llm-wiki native: web captures
│   ├── github/<slug>.md          # Pin-llm-wiki native: GitHub repo captures
│   ├── youtube/<slug>.md         # Pin-llm-wiki native: YouTube transcripts
│   ├── local/<slug>.md           # BCOS extension: from Path B (inbox-promoted or user-provided)
│   └── <type>/_manifest.md       # Per-type manifest (fetch date, source URL/path, hash) — same pattern as _collections/<type>/_manifest.md
│
└── .archive/                     # Soft-deleted pages with dangling-reference scan history
    └── <archived-slug>.md
```

**Why no per-page-type sub-folders.** BCOS's standard is "flat in `docs/` root with `cluster:` frontmatter for grouping, not subdirectories." Per-page-type folders (`how-to/`, `runbook/`, etc.) would duplicate the `page-type:` discriminator and force **five places to touch** when adding a new page-type: (1) frontmatter enum, (2) folder, (3) lint rules, (4) schema docs, (5) README. Flat keeps `page-type:` as the single source of truth — adding a new page-type is one schema edit. Folder is convenience; frontmatter is authority.

**Why `source-summary/` keeps its own folder.** Source-summary pages are the only ones that have umbrella/sub/unified topology, are produced by a different ingest path, follow a different review cadence, and aligns with pin-llm-wiki's upstream layout. Co-locating them with internal pages would hide that asymmetry.

**Why `queue.md` not `inbox.md`.** Pin-llm-wiki's `inbox.md` is a single-file URL queue; BCOS's `_inbox/` is a folder for triage. Different concepts, distinguishable names.

**Why `raw/` is nested inside `_wiki/`.** Raw captures exist *because* of wiki ingest. `_collections/` is for user-uploaded evidence with value independent of any wiki page. Co-location keeps the wiki ecosystem self-contained.

**`index.md` is a derived artifact, not authored truth.** It is a registry of pages — title, page-type, `builds-on`, `last-reviewed` — regenerated from frontmatter by `refresh_wiki_index.py` (shipped with the framework, wired into `job-index-health.md`). Hand-editing is wrong; if `index.md` looks stale, the fix is to run the refresh script, not to edit the file. This follows the same derived-artifact pattern as `.claude/quality/ecosystem/state.json` (lesson `L-INIT-20260404-009`: *"State files record what discovery found, not the definition itself."*). `overview.md` and `log.md` are NOT derived — they are authored by ingest (cross-source synthesis + append-only history).

## Frontmatter Schema

BCOS's required fields, plus wiki-only discriminators. All fields kebab-case.

```yaml
---
# ─── BCOS-required (same bar as data points) ────────────────────────────
version: 1.0.0
created: 2026-04-30                # immutable
last-updated: 2026-04-30           # bumped on every change
cluster: brand-identity            # primary cluster — must match an entry in cluster vocabulary (see Schema)
owner: gunti
status: active                     # value must come from .schema.yml `statuses:` list

# ─── BCOS ownership spec (Level 1 minimum) ──────────────────────────────
domain: "How to apply our brand voice on LinkedIn"
exclusively-owns:
  - LinkedIn-specific tone adjustments
  - LinkedIn formatting conventions
strictly-avoids:
  - The brand voice itself (lives in brand-voice.md)
  - Audience definitions (live in target-audience.md)

# ─── BCOS relationships (replaces pin-llm-wiki's `related:` and `tags:`) ─
# See "Reference format rule" — slugs intra-zone, paths cross-zone.
builds-on:                         # cross-zone (data points): relative paths with .md
  - ../brand-voice.md
  - ../target-audience.md
references:                        # intra-zone (other wiki pages): bare slugs, no .md
  - linkedin-best-practices
provides: []                       # rare for wiki — usually consumers, not producers

# ─── Wiki-specific ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
page-type: how-to                  # value must come from .schema.yml `page-types:` registry
last-reviewed: 2026-04-30          # separate from last-updated; resets on human review

# ─── Provenance (NEW — for Path B local content; mirrors queue.md DSL for URLs) ──
provenance:
  kind: inbox-promotion            # url-fetch | inbox-promotion | local-document
  source-path: docs/_inbox/2026-04-15_acme-onboarding-notes.md  # original location
  captured-on: 2026-04-30
  promoted-by: gunti
  notes: ""                         # optional

# ─── Pin-llm-wiki shape discriminators (mutually exclusive — see "Three Structural Shapes") ──
# These are populated ONLY for page-type: source-summary
source-url: https://...
companion-urls: []
raw-files: []
subpages: []
parent-slug: null
detail-level: standard             # value must come from .schema.yml `detail-levels:` list
last-fetched: 2026-04-30
---
```

**Page-type-specific frontmatter rules** are defined in `_wiki/.schema.yml` (next-section). The frontmatter validation hook (PostToolUse on Edit/Write) reads the schema at validation time, so adding a new page-type is one schema edit — no code change.

## Citation Rules

Adopt pin-llm-wiki's banner; combine with BCOS's link-don't-duplicate discipline.

**Banner citation** (top of every wiki page, immediately after H1 if any):

- For `page-type: source-summary`:
  > *All claims below are sourced from `../raw/<type>/<slug>.md` unless otherwise noted.*
- For all other page types:
  > *Canonical knowledge: [[brand-voice]] | [[target-audience]] — see those for the source of truth.*

**Per-paragraph inline citation** only when a paragraph deviates from the banner sources.

**Wikilink discipline:**
- `[[wiki-slug]]` — references to other wiki pages (zone-internal graph)
- Markdown links `[text](../brand-voice.md)` — references to data points (cross-zone)
- Both are graph edges. Lint can reverse-build a citation graph from these.

**Reference format rule (normative — enforced by the frontmatter validator and lint):**

| Where | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Intra-zone references in frontmatter (`references:`, `subpages:`, `parent-slug:`) | Bare slug, no `.md` | `linkedin-best-practices` |
| Intra-zone references in body | Slug wikilink `[[...]]` | `[[brand-voice]]` |
| Cross-zone references in frontmatter (`builds-on:` to data points) | Relative path with `.md` | `../brand-voice.md` |
| Cross-zone references in body | Markdown link with relative path | `[brand voice](../brand-voice.md)` |
| Raw-file references (`raw-files:`) | Relative path with `.md` | `../raw/web/example-com.md` |

Slugs survive renames within the zone (refactor-safe via `/wiki schema rename`); paths are necessary cross-zone because data points live in `docs/`, not `_wiki/`. Mixing the two — e.g., putting `.md` on intra-zone references — is a lint error (`reference-format-mismatch`).

**Hard rule:** a wiki page that *restates* a fact owned by a data point is a lint error (`duplication-vs-data-point`). Replace the restatement with a wikilink/markdown-link.

## Three Structural Shapes (source-summary only)

Pin-llm-wiki's clean topology for external sources, kept verbatim. Discriminators are mutually exclusive — lint enforces this.

| Shape | Discriminator(s) | When | Example |
|-------|------------------|------|---------|
| **Standalone** | none of the below | Single source, single page | YouTube talk; one-product website |
| **Unified (web+github)** | `companion-urls:` + `raw-files:` (multiple) | One product across a website *and* its companion repo | langchain.com + github.com/langchain-ai/langchain |
| **Umbrella + sub** | umbrella has `subpages: [...]`; subs have `parent-slug:` | One domain, multiple products | langchain.com → langgraph, langsmith |

**Lint check `frontmatter-shape`:**
- A page MUST be exactly one shape.
- Subs must point to an existing umbrella (`parent-slug` resolves).
- Umbrellas must not declare `companion-urls:` or `raw-files:`.
- Unified pages must not declare `subpages:` or `parent-slug:`.
- These rules apply only when `page-type: source-summary`.

## Two Ingest Paths

There are **two** real paths through the wiki ingest pipeline, distinguished by where the content originates from. Both converge on the same page format, the same lint, the same `log.md` entry.

### Path A — URL via queue (pin-llm-wiki native)

**Trigger:** "Add this URL to the wiki" / a URL appears in `docs/_wiki/queue.md`.

**Flow:** `queue.md` accumulates URLs → `/wiki run` (batch) or `/wiki run <url>` (single) → source-type detection (web/github/youtube) → fetch via `gh`, `yt-dlp`, or `WebFetch` → write verbatim capture to `_wiki/raw/<type>/<slug>.md` → ingest into `_wiki/source-summary/<slug>.md` → update `overview.md` and `log.md` → run `refresh_wiki_index.py` (regenerates `index.md` from frontmatter) → mark queue line `<!-- ingested YYYY-MM-DD -->`.

**Use it for:** external knowledge. The truth lives somewhere else and we want a citable, durable copy plus a wiki page.

### Path B — Local content (covers `_inbox/` promotions AND direct uploads)

**Trigger:** any local content the user wants in the wiki — pasted text, drag-dropped PDF/.docx, or a previously-captured file in `docs/_inbox/`.

**Flow:** `/wiki create from <path-or-paste>` → if path starts with `docs/_inbox/`, treat as inbox-promotion; if path is anywhere else (or content is pasted), treat as local-document; if file is binary (PDF, .docx, .zip), the binary lands at `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.<ext>` AND a markdown extraction lands at `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.md`; if pasted text or text file, it goes straight to `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.md` → page authored at `_wiki/pages/<slug>.md` with full BCOS frontmatter including `provenance:` block recording the origin → standard finalization → if origin was `_inbox/`, the original `_inbox/` file is deleted (its content now lives immutably in `raw/local/`).

**Wiki ingest never writes to `_collections/`.** `_collections/` is the user's evidence zone — files-as-received with value independent of any wiki page. If the user explicitly wants the binary to also live in `_collections/` as standalone evidence (e.g., a signed contract that's also the source for a wiki how-to), that's a separate `/collections add` operation. The wiki page can then `builds-on:` the `_collections/` path. Implicit cross-zone writes blur the boundary; explicit ones don't.

**Why one path, not two.** The earlier draft split inbox-promotion and local-document into separate paths. They share 95% of the pipeline; the only real difference is the trigger. Collapsing to one path with `provenance.kind:` distinguishing the trigger is simpler. The triage signal coming *into* Path B can vary; the path itself doesn't.

**Triage signals during `context-ingest` that suggest Path B (wiki-bound):**
- Content describes a **how**, **process**, or **steps** rather than a fact
- Content is a **post-incident narrative**
- Content is a **glossary-shaped definition list**
- Content is a **decision narrative** (the why behind a choice)

### Path Comparison

|                          | Path A (URL via queue) | Path B (local content) |
|--------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Source of content        | External web | Local file, paste, or `_inbox/` |
| Verbatim copy lives in   | `_wiki/raw/<type>/` | `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.md` (text) and `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.<ext>` (binary, if any) |
| Wiki page lives in       | `_wiki/source-summary/` | `_wiki/pages/` |
| Provenance carrier       | HTML-comment DSL on queue.md line | `provenance:` frontmatter block on the page |
| Three structural shapes  | Yes (umbrella/sub/unified) | No |
| Auto-discovery (companion repo, llms.txt) | Yes | No |
| Triggered by             | `/wiki run` or queue.md edit | `context-ingest` triage → `/wiki create from` |

## The HTML-Comment DSL on `queue.md` (Path A lifecycle)

Pin-llm-wiki's best mechanic: encode the entire lifecycle of a URL on one Markdown line, git-diffable.

```markdown
## Pending
- [ ] https://blog.langchain.dev/state-of-ai-agents/         <!-- detail:deep -->
- [ ] https://github.com/langchain-ai/langchain              <!-- detail:standard branch:main clone -->
- [ ] https://youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g                <!-- detail:standard -->
- [ ] https://example.com/docs                               <!-- companion:github.com/example/repo -->
- [ ] https://broken.example.com                             <!-- skip note:404 last checked 2026-04-15 -->

## Completed
- [x] https://anthropic.com/news/claude-3-7                  <!-- ingested 2026-04-22 -->
- [x] https://github.com/karpathy/llm-os                     <!-- ingested 2026-04-18 refresh -->
- [x] https://example.com/old-docs                           <!-- ingested 2026-03-30 -->
```

**Tag vocabulary:**

| Tag | What it does |
|-----|--------------|
| `<!-- detail:brief\|standard\|deep -->` | Per-source detail-level override |
| `<!-- branch:NAME -->` | GitHub: override default branch |
| `<!-- clone -->` | GitHub deep mode: full `git clone` to `raw/github/<slug>/` |
| `<!-- companion:URL -->` | Web: override companion-repo discovery |
| `<!-- no-companion -->` | Web: suppress companion fetch |
| `<!-- skip -->` | Mark line to skip on every run until removed |
| `<!-- refresh -->` | Re-fetch on next run |
| `<!-- note: ... -->` | User freeform comment |
| `<!-- fetch-failed:REASON -->` | Auto-added on failure |
| `<!-- ingested YYYY-MM-DD -->` | Auto-added when moved to Completed |

The queue is the state machine. Git history shows every transition without a separate registry file.

## Provenance for Path B (local content)

Path B has no equivalent of the queue.md state machine because there's no queue — the content arrives via direct command. Instead, **every Path B page carries a `provenance:` frontmatter block** that records the origin in a git-diffable way:

```yaml
provenance:
  kind: inbox-promotion            # url-fetch | inbox-promotion | local-document
  source-path: docs/_inbox/2026-04-15_acme-onboarding-notes.md
  captured-on: 2026-04-30
  promoted-by: gunti
  notes: "From discovery call with Acme on 2026-04-15"
```

**Refresh logic for Path B content:**

- **`kind: inbox-promotion`**: source has been deleted from `_inbox/` after promotion. Refresh = N/A; the raw capture in `_wiki/raw/local/` IS the durable copy. To "refresh," user provides new content via `/wiki create from <path>` and existing page is updated, with `last-updated` bumped and an entry in `log.md`.
- **`kind: local-document`** (file still on disk somewhere accessible): refresh re-reads the source file, hash-compares against the raw capture, and updates the wiki page if changed. If the file is no longer accessible, lint reports `provenance-source-missing` (INFO).
- **`kind: url-fetch`**: see "Refresh-Must-Rediscover" below; this kind only appears on `_wiki/source-summary/` pages.

The `provenance:` block makes Path B's lifecycle just as auditable as Path A's queue.md.

## Detail Level as Cost Knob

Three levels with explicit cost expectations — forces honest tradeoffs at queue time. Values come from `_wiki/.schema.yml` `detail-levels:` list (default `[brief, standard, deep]`).

| Level | Web | GitHub | YouTube | Approx tokens | Approx cost (Sonnet) |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| `brief` | Landing page only | README only | Metadata + chapters; no transcript | 5–15k | <$0.05 |
| `standard` | Landing + 4–10 docs pages | README + key docs files | Metadata + chapters + transcript | 30–80k | $0.10–0.30 |
| `deep` | Full docs crawl + companion repo discovery + multi-product split | README + docs/ tree + (optional) full clone | Standard + deep transcript scrub | 100–500k+ | $0.50–3.00 |

**Token guard at 200k:** any single fetch that would cross 200k tokens halts and asks the user to confirm or downgrade detail level. Generalized to BCOS bulk operations (`context-mine` on long transcripts, `context-audit` on 100+ doc bases).

## Operational Discipline — Sub-Agent Isolation

Big-body wiki work happens in [`.claude/agents/wiki-fetch/AGENT.md`](../../../.claude/agents/wiki-fetch/AGENT.md), not in the main thread. The principle: **expensive reads should produce small returns**. A 100k-token URL fetched into main saturates the context window before a single page lands; the same is true for deep PDFs, .docx exports, and full-tier source-summary refreshes.

Every Path-A and Path-B body fetch dispatches via the Task tool with the `wiki-fetch` sub-agent. The sub-agent does the read + extract + suggest, writes the raw body to disk under `_wiki/raw/<type>/`, and returns a structured ≤4000-token summary (title, h2-outline, key-sentences, suggested-page-type, suggested-cluster, raw-file-pointer, citation-banner-fields). Main never holds the full body.

| Path | Operation | Sub-agent kind | What main thread receives |
|---|---|---|---|
| Path A — `/wiki run` | URL fetch (web/github/youtube) | `web` / `github` / `youtube` | Structured summary; raw at `_wiki/raw/<type>/<slug>.md` |
| Path A — `/wiki refresh` full-tier | Re-fetch source-summary | matches existing source-type | Structured summary + delta; main compares against stored frontmatter |
| Path B — `/wiki promote` (binary) | PDF/.docx extraction | `pdf` / `docx` | Structured summary; raw at `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.md` (binary preserved at `<slug>.<ext>`) |
| Path B — `/wiki create from <path>` | Local file extraction | `pdf` / `docx` / `local` | Structured summary; raw at `_wiki/raw/local/<slug>.md` |

**Cumulative budget guard.** Across N parallel sub-agent invocations in `/wiki run` batch mode, the calling skill consults [`_wiki_budget.decide_dispatch_strategy()`](../../../.claude/scripts/_wiki_budget.py) before dispatching. The rule: cumulative projected return tokens (N × 4000) must stay ≤ 80% of main-context limit. Above that, sub-agents serialize — the next batch waits for the previous batch's results to free up budget.

**Result validation.** Every sub-agent return passes through [`_wiki_fetch_contract.validate_result()`](../../../.claude/scripts/_wiki_fetch_contract.py) before the calling skill writes anything. Malformed outputs (missing required fields, oversized payload, type mismatches) are rejected with a clear error rather than producing a malformed wiki page.

**The anti-inline-fetch rule.** `run.md` Step 4 contains an explicit "do not fetch inline" directive. If a future edit brings inline-fetching back, [`test_wiki_fetch.py::WikiFetchSkillDispatchWiringTests`](../../../.claude/scripts/test_wiki_fetch.py) fails — the wiring discipline is mechanically enforced.

The 15-test regression at `test_wiki_fetch.py` covers: agent contract shape, dispatch wiring across run/refresh/promote/create, cumulative budget math, and result-schema validation across canonical / error / malformed / oversized variants.

## Refresh-Must-Rediscover (Path A only)

When a `source-summary` page is **fully** refreshed, the fetch protocol **must** re-run discovery from scratch — not assume the prior topology is still valid:

- A site that was single-product may have launched a second product → must re-detect multi-product split
- A site that was unified may have moved docs to a separate repo → must re-detect
- A landing page may now link to an `llms.txt` it didn't have before → must re-check

This is an anti-shortcut rule. Without it, refresh becomes "re-fetch the URLs we already had," and the wiki silently drifts from the source's actual structure. If topology changed, the prior wiki page is superseded (status: archived; new page(s) created).

**Two-tier cadence (token-cost optimization).** Full rediscover is expensive. To bound cost on large wikis, the `wiki-source-refresh` job runs in two tiers:

1. **Quick-check tier** (default cadence: `stale_threshold_days / 4`, e.g. 7 days when the default is 28). Issue an HTTP HEAD against the umbrella URL via [`_wiki_http.head_check`](../../../.claude/scripts/_wiki_http.py). Compare the returned `etag` / `last-modified` against the values stored in the source-summary page's frontmatter via `head_signals_unchanged()`. If unchanged, bump `last-fetched` and skip. If validators differ or are missing, **escalate to full rediscover immediately, regardless of where we are in the default tier.** The stored `content-hash` (schema 1.1) is NOT consulted at this tier — a body hash is a full-tier validator, not a HEAD-response signal.
2. **Default tier** (`stale_threshold_days`, e.g. 28 days). Full refresh-must-rediscover. After `wiki-fetch` returns the body, the main thread calls [`_wiki_http.body_content_unchanged()`](../../../.claude/scripts/_wiki_http.py) against the stored `content-hash` — this catches the "ETag drifted but bytes identical" case. Runs whether or not the quick-check has fired in the interim.

The HEAD-check is a *freshness signal*, not a substitute for rediscover. Topology changes (new sub-products, llms.txt added, companion repo split) often happen without ETag/Last-Modified moving — so the default tier catches them. Weak ETags (`W/"..."`) are normalized for comparison; servers that omit ETag fall back to Last-Modified; missing both validators escalate to full rediscover (the helper never auto-decides "unchanged" without evidence).

> **Layered validators (schema 1.1):** `etag` + `last-modified` are HEAD-tier (cheap, no body); `content-hash` is full-tier (computed once a body is fetched). They live in the same source-summary frontmatter but answer different questions and run at different cadences.

A manual `<!-- refresh -->` queue tag always triggers the default tier (skips quick-check).

## Maintenance Pipeline

### Audit of pin-llm-wiki's 12 lint checks

| pin-llm-wiki check | Verdict | Why |
|--------------------|---------|-----|
| #1 Citation coverage | **Keep** + extend | Banner cites must include `builds-on` data points for derivative pages |
| #2 Contradictions (deferred) | **Replace** | Use BCOS's existing 5-path contradiction handling from `context-ingest` |
| #3 Orphans | **Keep** | Page with zero inbound `[[wikilinks]]` and no edits in 12 months → archive candidate |
| #4 Missing wikilinks | **Keep** + extend | Also flag missing markdown-links to data points |
| #5 Stale sources | **Replace** | Use propagation-based: `builds-on.last-updated > self.last-reviewed` |
| #6 Terminology collisions | **Replace** | Use BCOS `strictly-avoids` enforcement |
| #7 Frontmatter shape | **Keep** + merge | Move into PostToolUse hook so it runs on every save |
| #8 Citation path format | **Keep** | Relative paths must resolve from current page location |
| #9 Inbox consistency | **Keep** + extend | Apply to BCOS's `_inbox/` too (Path B left no orphans) |
| #10 Adapter sync | **Drop** | BCOS has one `CLAUDE.md`; no adapter problem |
| #11 Split-product | **Replace** | Use BCOS's "merge data points when…" guidance |
| #12 Parent-child consistency | **Keep** | Umbrella ↔ sub frontmatter must be consistent |

### New BCOS-specific checks

| Check | What it does | Severity |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| `duplication-vs-data-point` | Wiki page restates content found in a `builds-on` data point | WARN |
| `coverage-gap` | Active data point with no wiki explainer + frequent mention in `_inbox/` captures | INFO |
| `source-summary-upstream-changed` | `last-fetched > stale_threshold_days` AND HEAD check confirms upstream changed | INFO |
| `cluster-mismatch` | Wiki page's `cluster:` doesn't match its `builds-on` data points' `cluster:` | WARN |
| `forbidden-builds-on-target` | `builds-on` includes a path under `_planned/`, `_inbox/`, or `_archive/` (configurable in `.schema.yml`) | ERROR |
| `provenance-source-missing` | Path B `kind: local-document` page references a `source-path` that no longer exists on disk | INFO |
| `schema-violation` | Frontmatter value not in `.schema.yml` enum (e.g., `page-type: foo` when `foo` not registered) | ERROR |

- Run `refresh_wiki_index.py` to regenerate `_wiki/index.md` from frontmatter (replaces the obsolete "insert overview/log link" auto-fix — the regenerator always includes those links)
- Rewrite citation paths when data points are renamed (when redirect map provided)
- Mark queue lines `<!-- ingested YYYY-MM-DD -->` after successful Path A run
- Move expired post-mortems to `status: archived` when `auto-archive-after-days` (per-page-type schema setting) elapses

## Scheduled Jobs

Four jobs added to `.claude/quality/schedule-config.json` (and template). All wire into `schedule-dispatcher`.

```json
{
  "jobs": {
    "wiki-stale-propagation": {
      "enabled": true,
      "schedule": "daily",
      "_about": "When a data point's last-updated advances, flag every wiki page whose builds-on includes that data point as needs-review (until human resets last-reviewed)."
    },
    "wiki-source-refresh": {
      "enabled": true,
      "schedule": "weekly",
      "_about": "Two-tier refresh on source-summary pages. (1) Quick-check tier (default cadence: stale_threshold_days/4): HEAD-check upstream; if hash unchanged, bump last-fetched and skip. (2) Default tier (stale_threshold_days): full refresh-must-rediscover regardless of HEAD result — re-runs source-type detection, product-discovery, companion-repo discovery from scratch. HEAD-check is a freshness signal, not a substitute for rediscover."
    },
    "wiki-graveyard": {
      "enabled": true,
      "schedule": "monthly",
      "_about": "Pages with last-reviewed > 12 months AND no recent inbound traffic → AskUserQuestion about archive."
    },
    "wiki-coverage-audit": {
      "enabled": true,
      "schedule": "quarterly",
      "_about": "Daydream-adjacent. Two outputs: (1) which active data points lack a wiki explainer? (2) Vocabulary suggestions: any frequently-used `<!-- note: -->` keywords or recurring page tags that should be promoted to a registered page-type in .schema.yml?"
    }
  },
  "auto_fix": {
    "whitelist": [
      "wiki-index-refresh",
      "wiki-mark-queue-ingested",
      "wiki-archive-expired-post-mortem",
      "wiki-rewrite-citations-on-rename"
    ]
  }
}
```

**Cadence rationale:**
- **Daily** for `wiki-stale-propagation` — propagation events are the highest-value signal; cheap to compute (read frontmatter only, no fetches).
- **Weekly** for `wiki-source-refresh` — fetch cost is non-trivial; daily is overkill, monthly is too slow. HEAD-check skips unchanged sources to keep the cost bounded.
- **Monthly** for `wiki-graveyard` — slow-moving by nature; running daily would generate noise.
- **Quarterly** for `wiki-coverage-audit` — daydream-territory, expensive synthesis; the vocabulary-suggestion sub-check needs accumulated usage data.

All cadences and thresholds (graveyard age, stale-day count, coverage-audit lookback) are tunable per repo via `_wiki/.schema.yml` and `_wiki/.config.yml`. The `schedule-tune` skill is the user-facing surface for changing them.

## Inbox Reconciliation

Two inboxes is the wrong answer. The reconciliation:

- **`docs/_inbox/`** stays the unified BCOS triage zone for *anything* unclassified
- **`docs/_wiki/queue.md`** is an *intentional* URL queue (Path A only)
- **`context-ingest`** gets Path 7 in its triage tree: when classifying an `_inbox/` item as wiki-bound, it hands off to `/wiki create from <_inbox/path>` (Path B with `kind: inbox-promotion` provenance), or extracts URLs from the capture and routes them into `_wiki/queue.md`

This preserves the single-entry-point principle while letting the wiki own its specialized URL pipeline.

## Boundary with `lessons.json`

BCOS's `lessons.json` (maintained by `lessons-consolidate`) and the wiki's `post-mortem` and `decision-log` page-types overlap in domain (institutional knowledge). The boundary:

| | Lives in `lessons.json` | Lives in `_wiki/pages/` |
|---|---|---|
| **Form** | Atomic, structured: one principle + tags + provenance | Long-form narrative: multi-paragraph story |
| **Length** | One to three lines | Page-length |
| **Question it answers** | "What rule should I follow?" | "What happened? What did we decide and why?" |
| **Plural?** | Many small entries | One narrative per incident/decision |
| **Example** | "Always check Stripe webhook signatures before processing — see q1-pricing-incident" | The full q1-pricing-incident.md post-mortem |

**The flow:** a post-mortem (wiki page) is a narrative source. Reading the post-mortem produces zero or more atomic lessons that `lessons-consolidate` extracts into `lessons.json`. The lesson cites the post-mortem in its provenance; the post-mortem links back via wikilink/reference.

`daydream` and `wiki-coverage-audit` cross-reference both: a wiki post-mortem with no extracted lessons is a candidate for `lessons-consolidate`; a recurring lesson that points to no post-mortem is a candidate for new wiki-page authoring.

## Schema and Governance

Vocabularies in BCOS should grow with the business, not be chiseled in stone. The wiki zone exposes its vocabulary via a single registry file, **`_wiki/.schema.yml`**, that drives validation, lint, and tooling. Every type, category, and constraint lives in one place. Schema-versioned. Migrating between versions is a defined operation.

### `_wiki/.schema.yml` registry

```yaml
schema-version: 1
last-updated: 2026-04-30
last-migrated: null                  # set by `/wiki schema migrate`

page-types:
  how-to:
    description: "Procedural — steps for accomplishing a task"
    required-fields: [builds-on]
    review-cadence-days: 180
  glossary:
    description: "Term definitions for newcomers"
    required-fields: []
    review-cadence-days: 365
  source-summary:
    description: "External source captured via Path A (URL via queue)"
    required-fields: [source-url, last-fetched, detail-level]
    structural-shapes: [standalone, unified, umbrella-sub]
    review-cadence-days: 90
  # add page-types as you discover them; suggested next:
  # runbook, post-mortem, decision-log, faq, playbook, tutorial, meeting-notes

statuses: [active, under-review, archived]
detail-levels: [brief, standard, deep]
provenance-kinds: [url-fetch, inbox-promotion, local-document]

# Cluster vocabulary inherits from BCOS (single source of truth)
cluster-source: docs/document-index.md
allow-cluster-not-in-index: false    # strict; or true to permit wiki-only clusters

# Lint: forbid building on non-canonical sources
forbid-builds-on-paths:
  - _planned/
  - _inbox/
  - _archive/

# What `bcos init` should scaffold in a new repo
bootstrap-defaults:
  page-types: [how-to, glossary, source-summary]
  note: "Start small. Add page-types as you discover real categories you keep wanting."
```

### Governance operations

| Command | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| `/wiki schema list` | Show current vocabulary (read-only inspection) |
| `/wiki schema add page-type <name>` | Edit `.schema.yml`; `AskUserQuestion`-driven prompts for required-fields, description, cadence; lint validates; record in `log.md` |
| `/wiki schema rename page-type <from> <to>` | Migrate `page-type:` frontmatter on all matching pages; offer related rewrites; record migration in `log.md` |
| `/wiki schema retire page-type <name>` | Mark deprecated in schema; lint existing pages of that type with migration hint; doesn't delete |
| `/wiki schema validate` | Walk every page; verify against current schema; report violations |
| `/wiki schema migrate <from> <to>` | Versioned migration with dry-run diff before applying |

### Migration safety net (dry-run diff)

```
$ /wiki schema rename page-type how-to procedure

Migration plan:
  Update frontmatter:   12 pages       (page-type: how-to → page-type: procedure)
  Update schema:        .schema.yml    (page-types.how-to → page-types.procedure)
  Update lint config:   1 reference
  Update README:        2 mentions
  Log entry:            _wiki/log.md   (migration: schema 1 → 2)

Proceed? [Yes / Show diff / No]    ← AskUserQuestion
```

The log entry records the inverse migration so it's reversible.

### Bootstrap behavior on `bcos init` in a new repo

- `.schema.yml` is scaffolded with sensible defaults — initially just `how-to`, `glossary`, `source-summary` (the three universal categories)
- Header comment: *"Review this. Add page-types as you discover real categories you keep wanting."*
- The `wiki-coverage-audit` quarterly job gains a sub-check: **vocabulary suggestion** — *"You keep tagging pages with `<!-- note:meeting-notes -->`. Add `meeting-notes` as a registered page-type?"* Real-usage drives schema growth, not upfront speculation.

### Same pattern, other registries

For consistency across BCOS, the schema-driven approach applies beyond the wiki:

- **`_collections/.schema.yml`** — collection sub-types, manifest-schema column requirements, naming conventions, archive policy. See [`collections-zone.md`](./collections-zone.md).
- **`.claude/quality/lint-checks.json`** — registry of lint check IDs, severity, scope. New checks register here without code changes to the dispatcher.
- **`.claude/quality/schedule-config.json`** — already exists; we add a required `_about` field so the catalog is self-documenting.

The principle: **adding a category should be one edit, not five.**

## Retrieval — Today and Tomorrow

The wiki zone is now a read store, not just a write store. Mechanical cross-zone search shipped in P2 of the wiki-missing-layers plan (2026-05-04). The graph/embeddings layers below are still future-looking and ship behind P6 (conditional on real precision needs).

### What's shipped — mechanical cross-zone search (P2)

`/context search "<query>"` and the wiki-scoped sugar `/wiki search "<query>"` both run mechanical BM25-style ranking over [`.claude/quality/context-index.json`](../../../.claude/quality/context-index.json) — the canonical corpus that [`context_index.py`](../../../.claude/scripts/context_index.py) builds from `docs/`. The engine lives at [`context_search.py`](../../../.claude/scripts/context_search.py); the skill surface lives at [`context-routing/SKILL.md`](../../../.claude/skills/context-routing/SKILL.md) and [`bcos-wiki/search.md`](../../../.claude/skills/bcos-wiki/search.md).

Three rules are load-bearing:

1. **Mechanical-first (D-10 strict).** The default path is pure Python, deterministic, ms-latency. The engine never decides on its own to call the LLM. The only LLM-touching path is `--semantic`, which the user opts into explicitly. There is no auto-trigger on 0-hit, no fallback, no hidden cost.
2. **Stable citation-ids.** Every hit returns `citation-id: zone:slug` (e.g., `wiki:linkedin-tone`, `active:pricing`, `planned:wiki-missing-layers`). Path-derived; survives whitespace edits; cited back by other agents and by P5 bundle output.
3. **Empty zones are graceful.** When `_wiki/` is not initialized in a fresh repo, search returns `[]` for the wiki zone and surfaces it under `zones-skipped-not-present`. Not a hard failure.

The 15-test regression at [`test_context_search.py`](../../../.claude/scripts/test_context_search.py) covers the shared schema, ranking, zone filter, top-K cap, citation stability, the D-10 escalation rules, the empty-corpus path, and CLI smoke tests.

### Graph readiness

The frontmatter is already a typed-edge graph specification:

| Field | Edge type | Direction |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| `builds-on` | `DERIVES_FROM` | wiki → data-point or wiki → wiki |
| `references` | `CITES` | wiki → wiki (peer) |
| `provides` | `SUPPLIES_TO` | wiki → consumer (rare) |
| `raw-files` | `CAPTURED_FROM` | wiki → raw artifact |
| `companion-urls` | `UNIFIED_WITH` | source-summary → URL |
| `subpages` | `UMBRELLA_OF` | source-summary → source-summary |
| `parent-slug` | `SUB_OF` | source-summary → source-summary |
| `provenance.source-path` | `PROMOTED_FROM` or `EXTRACTED_FROM` | wiki page → original artifact path |
| `[[wikilink]]` in body | `MENTIONS` | wiki → wiki (with surrounding-text context) |

A graph generator can walk `_wiki/` + `docs/*.md` + `_collections/` and emit nodes (with frontmatter as attributes) + edges (with provenance). The Context Atlas (`.claude/scripts/bcos-dashboard/atlas_collectors.py`) already does this for data points; extending to wiki + collections is mechanical.

### Chunking readiness

- **Consistent H2 structure, no horizontal rules between sections** → each `## H2` is a chunk candidate
- **Banner citation at top** → every chunk inherits provenance via the page-level banner
- **Frontmatter is page-level metadata** → each chunk carries `cluster`, `page-type`, `builds-on`, `last-updated` for filtering
- **Stable kebab-case slugs** → chunk IDs don't change when content edits happen
- **No H1 inside body** (filename = title) → no ambiguity about which heading is the page title

### Embedding-update logic

Same trigger logic as `wiki-stale-propagation`:
- A data point's `last-updated` advances → re-embed every wiki page that `builds-on` it (chunk-granular)
- A raw file is overwritten → re-embed every wiki page whose `raw-files:` includes it
- Pages older than `last-reviewed > 12 months` flagged for re-embed regardless

The lint pipeline that flags review candidates IS the re-embedding queue.

### Provenance-grounded retrieval

A retrieval that returns a wiki chunk can cite, in order: (1) the chunk's section heading; (2) the page-level banner (`builds-on:` data points); (3) the page-level `raw-files:` (verbatim source); (4) the page-level `last-reviewed` (freshness). No chunk is orphaned from its provenance chain.

### What's deliberately NOT pre-built

We don't add explicit chunk-boundary markers, pre-computed embeddings, or a graph-store schema *now*. The data is already shaped so when those layers land, they slot in without re-formatting the existing wiki.

## Boundary with Strict Ownership

Wiki pages still declare `domain` + `exclusively-owns` (within the wiki's universe of explanation). A how-to owns "LinkedIn-specific tone adjustments" exclusively — no other how-to should claim that. But a wiki page's `exclusively-owns` is about *explanation*, not *canonical truth*. Two pages can explain the same data point from different angles (e.g., `linkedin-tone.md` and `customer-success-emails.md` both build on `brand-voice.md`) — they own different *applications* of the canonical truth.

The `duplication-vs-data-point` lint catches the failure mode where a wiki page accidentally tries to own canonical content (which already lives in a data point).
