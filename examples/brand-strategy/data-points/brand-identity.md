## Content

### Mission
To give mid-market teams the clarity and confidence to deliver their best work, without drowning in process.

### Vision
A world where mid-market companies have the same operational advantage as enterprises --- without the complexity, cost, or 6-month implementation timelines.

### Brand Name Usage
- Full name: **Acme Co** (used in formal contexts, legal, press releases)
- Short name: **Acme** (acceptable in marketing, casual references, and UI)
- Never: "ACME" (all caps), "Acme.co" (with a period), or "AcmeCo" (no space)
- The name is always followed by context on first mention in any public document: "Acme Co, the project management platform for mid-market teams"

### Founding Story
Acme Co was founded in 2020 by two operations leaders who spent years watching mid-market companies struggle with the same problem: enterprise project management tools were too complex and expensive, while simple tools couldn't handle the coordination needs of a 200-person company. After building internal tools at three different companies, they realized the gap wasn't about features --- it was about respecting the way mid-market teams actually work. Not with dedicated PMOs and certified project managers, but with team leads who juggle project management alongside their real jobs. Acme was built for them.

### Brand Values

1. **Clarity over complexity.** We believe the best tools make things simpler, not more complicated. Every feature we build must pass the "does this make someone's day easier?" test.

2. **Respect the real workflow.** Mid-market teams don't work like enterprises. We design for how people actually manage projects --- not how a textbook says they should.

3. **Progress, not perfection.** We would rather help a team ship at 80% than spend three months building the perfect Gantt chart. Done is better than theoretically optimized.

4. **Honest partnership.** We tell customers what our tool does well and where it doesn't fit. We would rather lose a deal than create a frustrated customer.

5. **Quiet confidence.** We don't need to be the loudest voice in the market. We let our customers' results speak for the product.

### Brand Personality Traits
- **Capable but not intimidating:** We know our stuff, but we don't make people feel small for not knowing theirs.
- **Opinionated but flexible:** We have a point of view on how project management should work, but we adapt to how teams actually work.
- **Warm but professional:** Friendly and human, not corporate or sterile, but never unprofessional or overly casual.
- **Direct but kind:** We say what needs to be said clearly, without jargon, and without being harsh.

### Brand Promise
When you use Acme, your team spends less time managing the process and more time doing the work that matters. We handle the coordination complexity so you don't have to.

## Context

### Strategic Implications
The founding story is our most powerful differentiation tool. We are not a project management company that decided to serve mid-market --- we were built by mid-market operators who lived the problem. This "built by, built for" narrative should be woven into every major brand touchpoint.

The values are not aspirational --- they describe how we already operate and should be used as decision-making filters. When a feature request conflicts with "clarity over complexity," the value wins. When a sales conversation tempts us to oversell, "honest partnership" is the check.

"Quiet confidence" is particularly important as a strategic differentiator. In a market full of tools shouting about "AI-powered project management revolution," we stand out by being calm, clear, and focused on results.

### Application Guidance
- **For marketing:** Use the founding story in about pages, investor decks, and PR. Lead with the problem (the mid-market gap), not the solution (our features).
- **For sales:** The "honest partnership" value is a closing tool, not a handicap. Customers trust us more when we say "we're not the right fit for that use case."
- **For product:** Use "clarity over complexity" as the primary design principle. Every new feature should be evaluated against it.
- **For hiring:** Brand personality traits should be reflected in job descriptions and the interview process. We hire people who are "capable but not intimidating."
