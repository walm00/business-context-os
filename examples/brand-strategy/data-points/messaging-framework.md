## Ownership Specification

**DOMAIN:** Key messages, taglines, proof points, and messaging hierarchy.

**EXCLUSIVELY_OWNS:**
- Primary brand message (the one sentence that captures what we do and why it matters)
- Secondary messages (supporting messages for different contexts)
- Elevator pitches (10-second, 30-second, 60-second versions)
- Proof points and evidence statements
- Channel-specific messaging variants (website, email, social, sales collateral)
- Messaging hierarchy (which messages lead in which contexts)
- Taglines and campaign lines

**STRICTLY_AVOIDS:**
- Brand voice and tone guidelines (see: brand-voice)
- Brand story and founding narrative (see: brand-identity)
- Audience demographics and segment definitions (see: target-audience)
- Product feature specifications (see: value-proposition)
- Competitive positioning strategy (see: competitive-positioning)

**BUILDS_ON:**
- brand-identity: Brand story, values, and promise shape the messaging hierarchy
- brand-voice: Voice guidelines determine how messages are written
- value-proposition: Value claims are translated into customer-facing messages

**REFERENCES:**
- target-audience: Audience segments determine message targeting
- competitive-positioning: Differentiation strategy shapes proof points
- customer-insights: Pain points inform which messages resonate most

**PROVIDES:**
- External teams (agencies, freelancers): Messaging framework is the primary brief for all external content creation

### Elevator Pitches

**10-second version:**
"Acme is project management built for mid-market teams --- powerful enough to coordinate across departments, simple enough that your team actually uses it."

**30-second version:**
"We help operations leaders at mid-market companies manage projects across teams without the complexity of enterprise software. Our customers tell us they chose Acme because their teams actually adopted it --- the average team hits 90% active usage within the first month. We are not the most feature-rich tool on the market, and that is by design."

**60-second version:**
"Mid-market companies have a project management problem that nobody talks about. You are too big for simple task managers but too lean for enterprise suites that require a dedicated PMO to run. Your team leads are managing projects as their side job, not their whole job. Acme was built specifically for this reality. We focus on coordination --- who needs what from whom, where handoffs are stuck, which projects need attention --- rather than creating elaborate tracking systems that nobody maintains. Our customers see 90% team adoption in the first month because we designed the tool around how mid-market teams actually work, not how a textbook says they should. That is why 800 companies trust us to keep their teams aligned."

### Secondary Messages

**For the operational decision-maker (primary audience):**
"Your team's project management tool should make their day easier, not add another system to maintain. Acme gives you visibility into what's happening across teams without burdening the people doing the work."

**For the team lead / power user (secondary audience):**
"Stop spending your mornings chasing status updates. Acme shows you exactly where things stand, who is waiting on what, and what needs your attention --- without a single status meeting."

**For the CFO / budget holder:**
"Acme replaces the patchwork of tools your teams are using with one platform at a fraction of enterprise pricing. Our customers report a 40% reduction in time spent on project coordination within the first quarter."

## Context

### Strategic Implications
The messaging hierarchy ensures consistency without rigidity. Every team member and agency partner should know which message to lead with in their context, but they have flexibility in how they express it.

The proof points are our most valuable messaging assets. Every claim is backed by a specific, verifiable number. This is rare in our market --- most competitors rely on vague claims like "teams love us" or "easy to use." Our specificity builds the trust that our "honest partnership" value promises.

The 10-second pitch is the most important message to get right. It is used more often than any other --- in networking, on calls, in Slack introductions. Everyone in the company should know it by heart.

### Application Guidance
- **For marketing:** Use the messaging hierarchy table to select the right lead message for every piece of content. Do not default to the primary message everywhere --- context-specific messages perform better.
- **For sales:** Customize the elevator pitches for the specific audience you are addressing. Use the audience-specific secondary messages as conversation starters.
- **For agencies and freelancers:** This document is your messaging brief. Do not invent new claims --- use the proof points provided. Voice and tone guidance is in brand-voice (separate document).
- **For everyone:** Update proof points quarterly. Stale numbers erode credibility faster than anything else.

## Maintenance

### Review Schedule
- **Regular review:** Quarterly (aligned with proof point updates)
- **Owner responsible for review:** Brand Lead

### Maintenance Triggers
- New customer milestone (e.g., reaching 1,000 customers)
- Updated proof point data from quarterly surveys or analytics
- New product launch that changes the value story
- Competitive repositioning that requires a messaging response
- Rebrand or campaign launch
- Proof points become stale (older than 6 months)
- Win/loss feedback reveals messaging gaps
