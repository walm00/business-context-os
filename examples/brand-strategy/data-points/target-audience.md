## Content

### Primary Audience: The Operational Decision-Maker

**Role titles:** VP of Operations, Director of Project Management, Head of Delivery, COO (at smaller companies)

**Who they are:** These are the people responsible for making sure work gets done across teams. They are not full-time project managers --- project coordination is one of many responsibilities. They typically manage 3-8 direct reports and have oversight of 15-50 people's workstreams.

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 32-48
- Experience: 8-20 years in operations, delivery, or general management
- Education: Bachelor's degree (60%), MBA or equivalent (30%), no degree but extensive experience (10%)

**Psychographics:**
- Pragmatists, not idealists. They want tools that work on Monday morning, not tools that look good in a demo.
- Skeptical of "transformational" software. They have been burned by tools that promised to revolutionize their workflow and ended up abandoned after 3 months.
- Value their team's time fiercely. They will reject any tool that adds overhead to their team's day, even if it gives leadership better reporting.
- Quietly ambitious. They want to be seen as the person who made operations run smoothly, not the person who bought a fancy tool.

**Buying behavior:**
- Research-heavy: They read reviews, ask peers, and run pilots before committing
- Risk-averse: They prefer proven solutions over cutting-edge ones
- Budget-conscious but not cheap: They will pay for value, but they need to justify every dollar to their CFO
- Decision timeline: 2-6 weeks for teams under 100, 6-12 weeks for larger organizations

### Secondary Audience: The Team Lead / Power User

**Role titles:** Team Lead, Project Coordinator, Senior Manager, Department Head

**Who they are:** The day-to-day users who manage projects inside Acme. They are often the ones who evaluate the tool during a pilot and whose adoption determines whether the purchase succeeds. They report to the operational decision-maker.

**Demographics:**
- Age range: 27-40
- Experience: 3-12 years
- Tend to be more digitally native than the primary audience

**Psychographics:**
- Frustrated by current tools (either too basic or too complex)
- Want to look competent and organized in front of their teams
- Willing to learn new tools if the payoff is clear within the first week
- Care more about daily workflow improvement than strategic reporting

### Firmographic Profile

**Company size:** 50-500 employees (sweet spot: 100-300)

**Revenue range:** $10M - $200M ARR

**Industries (by priority):**
1. Professional services (consulting, agencies, legal) --- 35% of current customers
2. Technology (SaaS companies, not building project management tools) --- 25%
3. Healthcare operations (clinics, hospital groups, health tech) --- 15%
4. Financial services (mid-size firms, fintech) --- 15%
5. Manufacturing and logistics operations --- 10%

**Company characteristics:**
- Has outgrown spreadsheets and basic tools (Trello, Asana basic) but finds enterprise tools (Monday.com Enterprise, Planview, Smartsheet Enterprise) overkill
- Does not have a dedicated PMO (Project Management Office)
- Typically has 3-10 concurrent projects running at any time
- Growing or recently grew (company went from 80 to 150 people in the last 18 months, and coordination broke)

## Context

### Strategic Implications
The primary audience's skepticism is both our biggest challenge and our biggest advantage. They have been burned by overpromising software before --- which means if we earn their trust, their loyalty is extremely strong. Our "honest partnership" brand value directly addresses this audience's core buying anxiety.

The firmographic sweet spot (100-300 employees) is critical. Below 100, companies can usually manage with simpler tools. Above 500, they start hiring PMO staff and looking at enterprise solutions. Our product, pricing, and messaging must all be calibrated for this range.

Professional services is our strongest segment because these companies have the most projects relative to their size and the clearest ROI from better project coordination. Growth-stage SaaS is our fastest-growing segment because they adopt quickly and refer within their networks.

### Application Guidance
- **For marketing:** Lead with the primary audience's pain ("managing projects is your side job, not your real job") rather than the product's features. Target the secondary audience through product-led content (templates, workflow guides) that they share with their boss.
- **For sales:** Identify whether you are talking to the decision-maker or the influencer early. Decision-makers care about team efficiency and cost justification. Influencers care about daily workflow and ease of use.
- **For product:** Design for the secondary audience (daily users) but report for the primary audience (strategic oversight). If the team leads love it, the VP will buy it.
